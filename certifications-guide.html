Small business certifications, explained

Being a women-owned, veteran-owned, or minority-owned business is a fact. Getting credit for it in grants, contracts, and set-aside programs usually requires a certification: a formal review that turns the fact into a document. This guide covers which certification does what, what each costs, and exactly where to start.

The honest first question: do you need one at all? Many private grants (the Amber Grant, most corporate giveaway-style grants) accept self-identification and require no certification. Certifications matter in three places: government contracting set-asides (federal and state), state programs that give certified businesses preference or exclusive eligibility, and corporate supplier diversity programs. If a grant's fine print says "certified," it will name which one. FundBug flags certification requirements on matched programs so you know before you apply.

SBA Women-Owned Small Business (WOSB / EDWOSB)

Federal · Free · Self-managed online

The federal certification for women-owned small businesses, run by the SBA. Required to compete for federal contracts set aside under the WOSB program; the EDWOSB tier adds economic-disadvantage criteria for a narrower set of contracts. Requires at least 51% ownership and control by one or more women who are U.S. citizens. Also accepted as proof of women-ownership by some grant programs.

Cost: FreeTypical timeline: a few weeks to a few monthsRenewal: annual attestation
Start at SBA.gov ↗

WBENC Women's Business Enterprise (WBE)

Private / corporate · Fee-based · Regional partner review

The most widely recognized private certification for women-owned businesses, used by hundreds of major corporations' supplier diversity programs and some private grant programs. Certification runs through regional partner organizations (in New England, the Center for Women & Enterprise) and includes a documentation review and site visit. If your growth plan involves selling to large corporations, this is the one they ask for.

Cost: fee scales with revenue (a few hundred dollars and up)Typical timeline: up to 90 daysRenewal: annual
Start at WBENC.org ↗

Massachusetts Supplier Diversity Office (SDO)

Massachusetts state · Free · The one MA businesses should look at first

The Commonwealth's own certification covering multiple designations: WBE (women), MBE (minority), VBE (veteran), PBE (Portuguese), DOBE (disability), and LGBTBE. SDO certification is what unlocks Massachusetts state contracting preferences and is referenced by various state and municipal programs. Importantly, the SDO offers an expedited path if you already hold a matching certification from WBENC, NMSDC, NGLCC, or the SBA, so the certifications stack rather than repeat.

Cost: FreeTypical timeline: varies; expedited with existing certsRenewal: periodic recertification
Start at Mass.gov SDO ↗

SBA Veteran Small Business Certification (VetCert: VOSB / SDVOSB)

Federal · Free · Moved to SBA in 2023

The federal certification for veteran-owned (VOSB) and service-disabled veteran-owned (SDVOSB) small businesses, now administered by the SBA (it previously lived at the VA, so older articles point to the wrong place). Required for VA and federal set-aside contracts for veteran-owned businesses, and accepted as proof of veteran ownership broadly.

Cost: FreeTypical timeline: about 30 to 60 daysRenewal: every 3 years
Start at SBA VetCert ↗

SBA 8(a) Business Development Program

Federal · Free · A nine-year program, not just a certificate

For small businesses at least 51% owned by socially and economically disadvantaged individuals. 8(a) is bigger than a certification: it's a nine-year development program with access to sole-source federal contracts, mentorship, and business development support. The application is substantial (personal financial statements, narratives) and the economic-disadvantage thresholds are real, but for businesses pursuing federal work it's the most powerful designation on this page.

Cost: FreeTypical timeline: several monthsTerm: one-time, 9 years
Start at SBA.gov ↗

SBA HUBZone

Federal · Free · Based on where you are, not who you are

The odd one out: HUBZone certification is about location, for businesses with a principal office in a Historically Underutilized Business Zone and at least 35% of employees living in one. Parts of Western Massachusetts and many rural areas qualify. Unlocks federal contract set-asides and price-evaluation preferences. Check the map first; if your address qualifies, this is free contracting leverage.

Cost: FreeFirst step: check the HUBZone mapRenewal: annual recertification
Start at SBA.gov (map included) ↗

NMSDC Minority Business Enterprise (MBE)

Private / corporate · Fee-based · Regional council review

The leading private certification for minority-owned businesses (at least 51% owned and controlled by U.S. citizens who are Asian, Black, Hispanic, or Native American), used by corporate supplier diversity programs nationwide. Certification runs through regional councils and includes documentation review and a site visit.

Cost: fee varies by region and revenueTypical timeline: up to 90 daysRenewal: annual
Start at NMSDC.org ↗

NGLCC LGBT Business Enterprise (LGBTBE)

Private / corporate · Free to apply · National council review

The national certification for LGBTQ+-owned businesses, recognized by hundreds of corporate partners' supplier programs and referenced by some grant programs. Massachusetts' SDO also recognizes it for its state-level LGBTBE designation, so this one does double duty in MA.

Cost: free applicationTypical timeline: 30 to 90 daysRenewal: annual
Start at NGLCC.org ↗

Disability:IN Disability-Owned Business Enterprise (DOBE)

Private / corporate · Certification for disability-owned businesses

The certification for businesses at least 51% owned, managed, and controlled by a person with a disability, including service-disabled veterans (V-DOBE). Used by corporate supplier diversity programs and recognized by the Massachusetts SDO for its state DOBE designation.

Typical timeline: 30 to 90 daysRenewal: annual
Start at Disability:IN ↗

Disadvantaged Business Enterprise (DBE)

Federal-state hybrid · Free · For transportation-funded work

Required for set-asides on federally funded transportation projects (roads, transit, airports). In Massachusetts, DBE certification runs through the state's Unified Certification Program administered via MassDOT. Only relevant if you work, or want to work, on public transportation-sector projects; if you do, it's the gate.

Cost: FreeAdministered by: MassDOT / state UCP
Start at Mass.gov ↗
